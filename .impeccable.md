## Design Context

### Users
Magic Compare is primarily for technical reviewers inside encoding groups and adjacent review workflows.
They use the internal workspace to organize cases and the compare viewer to inspect frame-level differences quickly and reliably.
Their core job is to judge before/after/heatmap changes with confidence under focused, task-oriented conditions.

### Brand Personality
The product should feel precise, calm, and crafted.
Its voice should be restrained, helpful, and matter-of-fact rather than playful or chatty.
The emotional target is confidence first, with subtle delight coming from polish, responsiveness, and visual discipline.

### Aesthetic Direction
Preserve the current dark-first editorial direction already present in the codebase: deep blue night surfaces, pale moon text, lavender and royal-blue accents, serif display headings, and IBM Plex Sans body copy.
Interfaces should feel like a focused review instrument rather than a generic SaaS dashboard or a flashy media toy.
Avoid loud cyberpunk neon, glassmorphism-heavy treatments, gray corporate flatness, and whimsical/game-like UI language.

### Design Principles
1. Comparison work stays primary; visual polish must never compete with the image review task.
2. Precision beats personality, but personality is welcome as subtle confidence-building polish.
3. First-use guidance should be brief, contextual, and disappear once learned.
4. Dark surfaces need clear boundaries, readable hierarchy, and obvious interaction states.
5. Responsive behavior must preserve review capability on every screen size instead of hiding core controls.
